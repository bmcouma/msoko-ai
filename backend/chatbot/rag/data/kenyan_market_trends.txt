KENYAN MARKET TRENDS & BUSINESS GUIDELINES (2026)

1. RETAIL & COMMODITIES (MAMA MBOGA / KIOSKS)
- Maize Flour (Unga): Wholesale prices have stabilized around KES 1,600 per bale (12 packets). Retail margin is tight; focus on fast turnover.
- Cooking Oil: Sourcing in bulk (20L jerricans) and dispensing in smaller quantities yields up to 30-35% profit margin.
- Fresh Produce: Sourcing directly from Marikiti or Wakulima Market very early morning (5 AM) provides the best wholesale pricing. Avoid weekend restocking as prices spike. 
- Fast Moving Consumer Goods (FMCG): Milk, bread, and sugar bring daily foot traffic. Don't overprice them; they are trap-products that lead to higher-margin impulse buys.

2. FREELANCE & DIGITAL SERVICES
- Global Positioning: For Kenyan tech freelancers, the global starting rate for web development (React/Django) is $20-$35/hr. Highlight the 2-hour timezone overlap with Europe to win UK/EU contracts.
- Digital Marketing: Local SMEs are willing to pay KES 15,000 - 30,000 monthly for social media management if ROI (leads) can be proven.

3. LOGISTICS & OPERATIONS
- Delivery: Boda boda delivery costs within Nairobi average KES 150-300. Businesses should negotiate flat monthly retainer agreements with 2-3 reliable riders to cut costs.
- M-Pesa Integration: Customers appreciate businesses that absorb Lipa na M-Pesa fees for transactions above KES 2,000. It builds extreme loyalty.
- Seasonal Peaks: December holidays trigger a 40% increase in electronics and fashion sales. Stocking up by early November avoids the wholesale price surge.
